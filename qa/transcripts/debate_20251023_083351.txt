Based on the documentation provided, here's an explanation of the **Casual Timesheet Extension (E027) Requirement** for the Queensland Department of Education:

## Overview
This is a **System Design Specification** (E027) for extending casual timesheet functionality within the SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll (ECP) system as part of the Queensland Department of Education's HCMS project.

## Key Requirements

### **Core Functionality**
- **Extension of existing timesheet capabilities** specifically for casual employees
- Enhanced time recording and management features for casual workforce
- Integration with payroll processing through ECP

### **System Components**
- **Data Model**: New data structures on Business Technology Platform (BTP) to support casual timesheet extensions
- **UI Design**: User interface for managing casual timesheets
- **Validations**: Business rule validations for casual employment scenarios
- **Processing Logic**: Rules for handling casual time entries
- **Authorizations**: Security controls for accessing casual timesheet functions

### **Integration Requirements**
- **Payroll Integration**: Timesheet data flows to ECP for payroll processing
- **Award Interpretation**: System must handle award-based calculations for casual employees
- **Allowance Valuation**: Proper calculation and processing of casual employee allowances

### **Business Context**
- Serves the **Queensland Department of Education's** casual workforce
- Part of the broader **Human Capital Management System (HCMS)** program
- Addresses **flexible scheduling** and **time recording** needs for casual employees
- Includes **phased deployment** approach with consideration for historic data

### **Current Status**
- Documented in **Explore Phase**
- Version 1 completed by author Murray Burdan
- Awaiting approval from SAP Solution Lead/Architect

The requirement essentially aims to enhance the existing SuccessFactors ECP system to better manage and process timesheets specifically for casual employees, ensuring proper payroll calculations and compliance with award conditions.
