Thank you for these excellent and comprehensive questions. I'll address each of your concerns based on the provided context and best practices for enterprise integration solutions:

## Integration and Data Flow Questions

**Real-time synchronization conflicts:** The integration architecture would need to implement a master data management approach where Employee Central serves as the authoritative source for position attributes. When conflicts arise, the system should implement:
- Timestamp-based conflict resolution with audit trails
- Business rule prioritization (e.g., RCM position changes take precedence over transfer applications when creating conflicts)
- Automated reconciliation workflows that notify relevant stakeholders when conflicts cannot be resolved automatically
- Position lock mechanisms to prevent double-filling during transfer processing

**System outages and integration failures:** The solution should include:
- Asynchronous messaging queues to handle temporary failures
- Transaction rollback capabilities to maintain data consistency
- Manual intervention workflows for critical outages
- Status tracking that preserves application state during system downtime
- Notification systems to inform users of delays and expected resolution times

## Workflow Complexity and Edge Cases

**Multi-stakeholder approval hierarchy:** The bidirectional approval process extends to include:
- Configurable approval chains that can route to regional offices based on location
- Committee-based approvals for exception cases (medical, compassionate transfers)
- Escalation procedures when primary approvers are unavailable
- Parallel approval streams for complex transfers involving multiple jurisdictions
- Role-based overrides for emergency situations

**Cascading transfers:** The system handles chain reactions through:
- Position dependency tracking that identifies potential cascades
- Batch processing of related transfers to optimize outcomes
- Priority algorithms that consider the entire transfer network rather than individual applications
- What-if scenario modeling to predict and mitigate negative cascading effects

## Eligibility and Business Logic

**Multi-dimensional eligibility weighting:** The priority system uses weighted scoring:
- Service years (40% weight): Transfer points accumulated over 10-year history from HR Resource Group
- Regional priorities (30% weight): Rural/priority area needs and regional matching algorithms
- Exceptional circumstances (20% weight): Medical, compassionate, safety considerations
- Professional requirements (10% weight): Specialized skills, qualifications for specific positions
- Business rules can be configured to adjust weights based on current organizational needs

**Evergreen requisitions:** These are managed through:
- Configurable expiration periods (typically 12-18 months)
- Automatic renewal notifications to position holders
- Manual override capabilities for extended availability
- Integration with talent pool management to maintain active candidate interest

## User Experience and Security

**Delegation during critical periods:** The system supports:
- Pre-configured delegation chains that activate automatically when approvers are unavailable
- Temporary role assignments during peak transfer periods
- Mobile-enabled approvals to accommodate field-based approvers
- Emergency delegation protocols for time-sensitive transfers

**Annual system closure:** Exception handling includes:
- Emergency transfer protocols for critical situations
- Expedited approval workflows for urgent cases
- Manual processing capabilities during closure periods
- Clear communication protocols about available services during closure

## Scalability and Performance

**Performance benchmarks:** Based on Queensland Department of Education scale:
- Concurrent processing of 10,000+ applications during peak periods
- Sub-second response times for eligibility calculations
- Batch processing capabilities for bulk operations
- Cloud-based scaling to handle peak demand during transfer windows
- Integration latency maintained under 2 seconds for critical data exchanges

The custom BTP solution is specifically designed to address these complex scenarios that standard RCM cannot handle, with the integration architecture being critical to success. The evidence gaps identified in the documentation (detailed workflow mapping, integration specifications) will need to be addressed during the Realize-Design phase to ensure these capabilities are properly implemented.

The blocking nature of these integration dependencies highlights the critical importance of resolving these technical and business logic questions before implementation to ensure the system can handle the full complexity of Queensland's teacher transfer requirements.
